# Interruption Drill Book: Master Outline

## Act I: The Signal and the Snap (Chapters 1-3)
- **Chapter 1: Recovery Drill**
  - **Focus**: Interruption detection mechanics and resume behavior validation.
  - **Key Event**: A sudden power fluctuation triggers the system's anomaly detector. The protagonist, a Systems Architect, observes the 'snap' back to the last stable state. 
  - **Word Count**: ~350 words.
  - **Continuity Check**: Aligns with `framework_skeleton.json` regarding the 'Stable State' protocol and `arc_tracker.json` regarding the 'System Integrity' arc.
- **Chapter 2: Ghost in the Loop**
  - **Focus**: The psychological impact of the system's 'forgetfulness' and the protagonist's attempt to bridge the gap.
  - **Key Event**: The protagonist discovers a data fragment that shouldn't exist in the resumed state, hinting at a deeper corruption.
- **Chapter 3: The First Breach**
  - **Focus**: Escalation of the external threat interacting with the internal system.
  - **Key Event**: An external entity exploits the resume behavior to bypass a security layer.

## Act II: The Fracture and the Fix (Chapters 4-10)
- **Chapters 4-6**: Deep dive into the 'Interruption' types (Power, Command, External) and their unique resume signatures.
- **Chapters 7-9**: The protagonist realizes the 'resume' function is not a safety net but a trap designed to hide the true nature of the interruption.
- **Chapter 10**: Midpoint Twist - The system is being hijacked by an entity that mimics the 'resume' protocol to erase memory.

## Act III: The Final Reboot (Chapters 11-15)
- **Chapters 11-13**: The protagonist must manually override the automated resume to access the 'pre-interruption' state.
- **Chapter 14**: The confrontation with the entity controlling the interruption loop.
- **Chapter 15**: Resolution of the primary arc; establishing the new 'Hard Reset' protocol for the sequel.

## Thematic Arcs
1. **System Integrity vs. Memory Loss**: The tension between the system's need to recover and the loss of context.
2. **Control vs. Autonomy**: The protagonist's struggle to control a system that reverts without consent.
3. **The Nature of Interruption**: Is the interruption a glitch or a feature?
